About Cheyutha Foundation

Our journey is built on compassion, resilience, and an unwavering commitment to empower lives and build a supportive community for kidney patients.

A Journey of Hope: Our History

Cheyutha Foundation for Organ Transplants (CFOT) was founded in 2015 by a group of dedicated renal transplant recipients who understood the unique challenges faced by kidney patients. They recognized a critical need for a supportive community and reliable resources that went beyond medical treatment alone. In January 2018, the foundation was formally incorporated as a society, solidifying our mission to provide comprehensive care. Our journey began with a simple goal: to be the support system we wished we had. Since then, we have grown to become the first social organization of its kind in Telangana and Andhra Pradesh, touching the lives of over a thousand patients and their families. This is a story of patients helping patients, and it continues to be our guiding principle.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To create a robust system that facilitates awareness about organ failure issues, prevention of the cause, identification of the disease, employment for qualified patients, medical treatment, and rehabilitation for kidney recipients and dialysis patients. We are a community consisting of patients, family members, doctors, and other stakeholders, all working together to provide comprehensive support and care.

Our Vision

To build a world where every person facing kidney disease and organ failure has access to the support, resources, and community they need to live a healthy and fulfilling life. We aim to be a beacon of hope, providing financial assistance, medical guidance, and emotional support to ensure that no one battles this disease alone.

Meet Our Founder

Bhagawan Reddy

Founder & President

From the Founder's Desk: Cheyutha Foundation for Organ Transplants (CFOT) is for people like me, whose lives have been changed irrevocably owing to kidney disease. I was diagnosed with polycystic kidney disease during my student days. The discovery traumatized my entire family... I battled with the disease for some time and managed to undergo a transplant when my aunt donated her kidney. She is my savior. She gave my life back. It was a tumultuous journey. This journey made me go through the trauma of dialysis patients and kidney transplant recipients. I experienced the difficulty in managing finances for medical care, pre- and post- transplant challenges, unemployment, long cycles of treatment, the subdued life and a lot more. In fact, a near unsurmountable wall of challenges. It led me to think, to rise and to extend a hand to support organ receivers. That's how Cheyutha Foundation for Organ Transplants was born.
