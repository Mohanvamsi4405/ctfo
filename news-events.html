January 1, 2024

Kidney Health Awareness Event at ESI

By CFOT Team

We held a successful event at ESI, bringing together patients and leading doctors to raise awareness about kidney health. A panel of nephrologists, a transplant coordinator, and a dietician provided valuable insights on kidney diseases, transplants, and organ donation to 173 kidney transplant recipients and 32 dialysis patients. The event also featured the launch of the "Kidney Warriors" book and the "Cheyutha" website, along with an awards ceremony to honor the medical professionals and participants.

January 14, 2018

Collaborative Board Meeting in Mumbai

By Founder Bhagavan Reddy

A productive board meeting took place on January 14, 2018, in Mumbai. The meeting brought together the founders of the Kidney Warriors Foundation, the All India Organisation Working for Chronic Kidney Patients, and the Cheyutha Foundation for Organ Transplants. Discussions centered on addressing critical issues faced by kidney patients and planning to submit memorandums to health ministries to advocate for their needs. Key participants included Ms. Vasundhara Raghavan, CEO, Founder, and author, and a kidney donor.

October 1, 2024

Supporting Dialysis Patients with Essential Supplies

By Bhagawan Reddy

To help address the high costs of post-dialysis care, we distributed essential supplies, including fistula belts for blood flow control and injections for blood improvement. Thanks to the generous support of our founders' family members, we were able to provide these critical items to over 500 patients. Distributions were held at NIMS, Gandhi, Star, and Care Hospitals, as well as at government dialysis centers in various districts. Each patient received supplies worth ₹500.

February 15, 2020

Vanitha TV special on COVID-19

Vanitha TV special on COVID-19

A Vanitha TV program provided crucial public guidance on medicine availability, medical supplies, and helplines during the COVID-19 pandemic.

This segment from Vanitha TV, titled "During Corona.. How are the medicines?", was a vital public-service program. It brought together experts, doctors, and officials to address critical concerns about the availability and supply of medicines and medical equipment during the COVID-19 pandemic. The discussion also focused on how people could access help and information through various helplines, providing crucial guidance and reassurance to the public during a time of crisis.
